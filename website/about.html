The Ministry

About the Federal Ministry of Defence.

Established on 1st October 1958, the Federal Ministry of Defence (FMOD) is the apex policy organ of Nigeria's defence establishment — providing strategic, administrative and operational leadership across the Armed Forces of the Federal Republic.

Our Mandate

Strategic leadership of Nigeria's defence.

FMOD is the civilian face of Nigeria's military authority. The Ministry translates the policy direction of the President and Commander-in-Chief into the strategic, financial and administrative actions that keep the Armed Forces — and therefore the country — secure.

The Ministry is administered by the Honourable Minister of Defence, supported by the Honourable Minister of State and the Permanent Secretary, who is the chief administrative and accounting officer.

Our Mission

What we do, every day.

To provide administrative and support services, timely and effectively, that enable the Armed Forces of the Federal Republic of Nigeria to build and maintain a modern, compact, strong, professional, mission-capable and mission-ready military for the defence of the territorial integrity of Nigeria.

Our vision: A modern, professional and well-motivated Armed Forces, fully responsive to the security needs of Nigeria and its strategic interests in the African continent and the wider world.

Statutory Functions

What the Ministry oversees.

01

Policy Formulation

Articulating defence policy, the National Defence Policy and inter-ministerial security strategy on behalf of the Federal Government.

02

Defence Budgeting & Finance

Coordinating the defence budget, payment systems, procurement and the financial governance of the Armed Forces and parastatals.

03

Civil–Military Liaison

Acting as the institutional interface between the elected Government, Federal Executive Council and the Armed Forces.

04

Welfare Administration

Overseeing pensions, housing, medical services and resettlement for serving personnel, veterans and their dependants.

05

Recruitment & Training Policy

Setting standards and policies for recruitment, the NDA, AFCSC, NDC and other Defence training institutions.

06

International Defence Co-operation

Bilateral and multilateral relations including ECOWAS, AU and UN peacekeeping, and defence diplomacy.

Heritage

Six decades of service.

The Federal Ministry of Defence was established on 1st October 1958, two years before Nigeria's independence, to provide civilian oversight of the Royal West African Frontier Force, which became the Nigerian Armed Forces in 1960. The Ministry has evolved through periods of conventional warfare, peacekeeping leadership, democratic consolidation and contemporary asymmetric threats.

Today, the Ministry coordinates the work of four operational components — the Defence Headquarters, the Nigerian Army, the Nigerian Navy, the Nigerian Air Force — and a network of agencies including the Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA), the National Defence College (NDC), the Armed Forces Command & Staff College (AFCSC), the Defence Industries Corporation of Nigeria (DICON), the Nigerian Armed Forces Resettlement Centre (NAFRC) and the Military Pensions Board (MPB).
